Update 1 Sunday Apr 19, 2026 — ~6:00 PM ET ▾
Hormuz Fully Closed, Tankers Fired On, US Seizes Iranian Vessel — Talks Still On

Today saw the situation escalate sharply on multiple fronts simultaneously, even as both sides signaled they still want a deal. Here is the full picture as of Sunday evening:

  • Zero tankers transited Hormuz on Sunday, per shipping tracking data. The IRGC ordered all vessels to treat passage as "cooperation with the enemy," threatening to target any violating ship. Lloyd's List confirmed traffic has come to a complete halt.
  • US Navy seized the Iranian cargo ship TOUSKA in the Gulf of Oman. The USS Spruance (guided missile destroyer) intercepted the vessel after it ignored six hours of warnings attempting to breach the US naval blockade. US Marines are on deck. CENTCOM confirmed. Iran called it "an act of aggression" and "maritime piracy" and vowed retaliation.
  • Iran fired on ships from multiple nations. India's foreign ministry summoned Iran's ambassador after IRGC gunboats fired on two Indian-flagged vessels. UK MTO confirmed a tanker was fired on by two IRGC gunboats. Botswana- and Angola-flagged tankers were also turned back. France's CMA CGM confirmed one of its vessels received warning shots.
  • Trump threatened to destroy "every single Power Plant and every single Bridge in Iran" via Truth Social, calling the tanker firings a "Total Violation of our Ceasefire Agreement" — while simultaneously saying the US was having "very good conversations" with Tehran. Carrots and threats in the same post.
  • Oil gapping hard: Brent +7% to $96.88, WTI +7% to $90.33 as of Sunday afternoon. Friday's close was the lowest since March 10 on the brief reopening news. That entire move has now reversed. US national average gas hit $4.05/gallon. Energy Secretary Chris Wright told CNN it may not return below $3 "until next year."
  • Talks are still scheduled. VP Vance, Steve Witkoff, and Jared Kushner are confirmed traveling to Islamabad. Iranian sources told CNN their delegation arrives in Pakistan on Tuesday. Iran's National Security Council said it is "examining new US proposals." Iran's parliament speaker Ghalibaf framed the core standoff bluntly on state TV: "It is impossible for others to pass through the Strait of Hormuz while we cannot."
  • Both ceasefire clocks are ticking simultaneously. The 2-week US-Iran truce technically expires Wednesday Apr 22. The 10-day Israel-Lebanon ceasefire also expires around that window. Negotiators reconvene Tuesday with both deadlines hanging over them.

The read: both sides are maximizing leverage before the next round, not walking away from the table. Iran re-closing Hormuz and the US seizing a vessel are coercive signals. The weekend timing of the escalation — when markets are closed — suggests Iran is applying pressure without triggering an immediate market rout that would also hurt them. The Islamabad talks on Tuesday are the single most important event to watch this week.
